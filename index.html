After graduating with a dual bachelor's degree in History and Law, a master's degree in late modern history, and a master's degree in Technologies numériques appliquées à l'histoire, I worked at Inria as a research and development engineer on the DAHN project before becoming a PhD candidate.

I am a PhD candidate in digital humanities at Le Mans Université and ALMAnaCH's Inria, under the supervision of Anne Baillot and Laurent Romary. My research focuses on ground truth and transcription models. My PhD progress is available in a dedicated repository: phd.

In my free time, I enjoy going to the movies, reading various books, and visiting exhibitions.
